Before we pitch anything
Questions
to Align On
These six questions need clear answers before these packages are shown to the client. They affect how I scope my work, how I price my time, and how this collaboration is structured long term.
01
Do we want to show clients a fixed delivery timeline, or quote it per project?
A fixed timeline makes the packages easier to sell and sets clear expectations. Per-project gives us flexibility but requires more back-and-forth. Your call — I adapt either way.
02
What is my cut per package sold, and how does payment flow — client pays you or me directly?
I need to know my actual number before I can calibrate how much time and effort is reasonable per package. This needs to be in writing before we start.
03
If a client approaches me for work outside the podcast scope, like an event, a launch, or a one-off project. Does that go through Inbox Alchemy, or can I take it independently?
This isn't about competing — it's about knowing where the boundaries are so we both operate with clarity. Let's define this upfront so there are no awkward conversations later.
04
Am I client-facing, or do you handle all communication and I just deliver the work?
Client-facing means meetings, revision calls, expectation management — real billable hours. If you handle communication, the scope and pricing look different.
05
Who sets the final price the client pays — you or me? Do I have visibility on that number?
You're welcome to mark up my fee, that's your business. I just need transparency on the final number so I can calibrate quality and the relationship stays healthy.
06
When I shop on behalf of a client in Package 02, who holds and approves the product budget?
This money cannot pass through me informally. It needs a documented flow — client approval in writing before any purchase. Let's agree on a process so neither of us is exposed.
